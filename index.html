Module 1

Foundations of Entrepreneurial Finance & Venture Funding

1.4 · Share Capital & Wealth Formulas
Net Worth (Book Equity)
Net Worth = Equity Share Capital + Reserves & Surplus
Represents the book value of all equity. Also the denominator for Book Value per Share. Used in P/B valuation multiples. Reserves & Surplus = retained earnings from past profits.
Book Value per Share
Book Value/Share = Net Worth ÷ Shares Outstanding
The accounting value of one equity share. Benchmark for P/B ratio. Compare with market price to determine if a stock trades at a premium or discount to book.
Market Capitalisation
Market Cap = Market Price per Share × Total Outstanding Shares
The total market value of a company's equity. Reflects investor expectations about future earnings. Early-stage startups have no public market price — value is negotiated.
Module 2

Startup Valuation & Investment Decision Framework

2.3 · Accounting Valuation Multiples
Earnings Per Share (EPS)
EPS = Profit After Tax (PAT) ÷ Number of Equity Shares
PAT = net profit after all taxes and interest. EPS is the denominator for the P/E ratio and measures per-share profitability. Cannot be calculated for loss-making startups.
Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio
P/E = Current Market Price ÷ EPS
Compares market price to earnings. Higher P/E = higher growth expectations. Industry clustering: firms in the same sector share similar P/E bands. Unusable when EPS is negative.
Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio
P/B = Current Market Price ÷ Book Value per Share
Compares market price to net worth per share. P/B > 1 means the market values the company above its accounting assets. Common for banks and asset-heavy businesses.
Equity Value from Enterprise Value
Equity Value = Enterprise Value (EV) − Liabilities (Debt + Pref. Shares)
EV captures total firm value (debt + equity). Stripping out liabilities gives the Equity Value — the market value attributable purely to shareholders.
2.4 · Pre/Post-Money Valuation & Dilution
Post-Money Valuation
Post-Money Valuation = Investment Amount ÷ Equity % Acquired
The notional total value of the company after the new cash is injected. If an investor pays ₹2 Cr for 20%, Post-Money = ₹2 Cr ÷ 20% = ₹10 Cr.
Pre-Money Valuation
Pre-Money Valuation = Post-Money Valuation − Investment Amount
Value of the company's existing assets before new cash arrives. In the example above, Pre-Money = ₹10 Cr − ₹2 Cr = ₹8 Cr. Rising pre-money across rounds = market validation.
Required Equity % at Exit
Required Exit Equity % = Target Cash Return ÷ Exit Valuation
The investor's percentage stake needed at exit to hit their return target. e.g., if target = ₹16 Cr and exit value = ₹40 Cr, investor needs 40% at exit.
Entry Equity % (Dilution-Adjusted)
Entry Equity % = Target Exit Equity % ÷ (1 − Future Dilution %)
Accounts for future dilution from subsequent funding rounds. If the investor wants 40% at exit but expects 25% future dilution, they need 40% ÷ 75% = 53.3% at entry.
Module 3

Financial Modelling, Cash Flow Planning & Funding Estimation

3.3–3.5 · Centre Economics & Funding Formulas
Associate Capacity (Kareer Sciences)
Capacity = 160 hrs/month ÷ 16 hrs/client = 10 clients per associate
160 hrs = working hours per associate per month. 16 hrs = engagement hours required per client. One associate can handle a maximum of 10 clients per month.
Associates Required
Associates Required = Active Clients ÷ 10
Staff headcount is driven strictly by client acquisition, not by management preference. Rounding up is mandatory — you cannot have a fractional associate.
Monthly Revenue
Monthly Revenue = No. of Clients × Fee per Client (₹25,000)
100% of revenue collected in the same month (zero accounts receivable assumption). Scales linearly with client count up to the centre capacity cap of 150 clients.
Total Funding Requirement
Total Funding = (Peak Deficit × Centres) + (Infra Cost × Centres) + Tech Investment
Peak Deficit = highest cumulative cash shortfall per centre. Infra Cost = ₹26 L/centre. Tech Investment = ₹10 Cr one-time cost for the "Crazy" path only — breaks the linear scaling relationship.
Module 4

Term Sheets & Investment Deal Structuring

4.2–4.5 · Key Structuring Rules & Thresholds
Convertible Discount to Next Round
Conversion Price = Next Round Price × (1 − 20%)
Investors who accept a convertible note receive a 20% discount to the price discovered in the next qualified funding round. Compensates early investors for bearing higher risk before valuation is set.
Liquidation Preference — Non-Participating
Investor receives MAX( 1× or 2× Capital , Equity % × Sale Price )
Investor chooses the higher of their guaranteed return or their equity share of proceeds. Cannot double-dip — must pick one. Protects downside without capping upside if equity value is higher.
Liquidation Preference — Participating
Investor receives (1× or 2× Capital) PLUS (Equity % × Remaining Proceeds)
Investor gets their guaranteed multiple first, then also participates in the remaining proceeds proportionally. More aggressive — founder receives residual only after investor's double benefit.
Anti-Dilution: Full Ratchet
New Conversion Price = New (Lower) Round Price
In a down round, early investor's conversion price resets fully to the new lower price. Harshest for founders — maximum founder dilution. Investor's % stake increases to compensate for valuation drop.
Anti-Dilution: Weighted Average
New Price = (Old Shares × Old Price + New Shares × New Price) ÷ Total Shares
Blended average between old and new price. Fairer — dilution pain shared between founder and investor. Broad-based (includes all shares) is the market standard; narrow-based is more investor-friendly.
Term Sheet Exclusivity Window
Exclusivity Period = 30 to 90 days (legally binding)
The only legally binding clauses in an otherwise non-binding term sheet are: Exclusivity, Confidentiality, and Governing Law. All other investment terms remain negotiable until the SHA/SPA is signed.

Self-Liquidating Instrument Return

IPO > Trade Sale > Secondary Purchase > Buyback
IPO: highest return, highest cost/time. Trade Sale: fast, cheaper, founder exits. Secondary: founder retains control. Buyback: least preferred — capped at 25% by law and requires company cash.
Module 6

Startup Fundraising & Investor Engagement

6.1–6.2 · Cash Flow & Capital Hierarchy Formulas
Operating Cash Flow (OCF)
OCF = PAT + Depreciation & Amortisation + Interest − ΔWorking Capital
PAT is the starting point. Add back non-cash expenses (D&A) and financing costs (Interest). Subtract the increase in working capital trapped in operations. OCF > 0 means the business generates cash.
Funding Priority Hierarchy
Internal Accruals → Debt (8–18%) → Equity (most dilutive)
Equity is the most expensive form of capital — investors demand high returns and dilute ownership. Exhaust internal accruals and affordable debt before raising equity. Add 10–15% contingency buffer to any capital estimate.
Market Sizing Framework
TAM ⊃ SAM ⊃ SOM   (nested subsets)
TAM = Total Addressable Market (global potential). SAM = Serviceable Addressable Market (target geography/segment). SOM = Serviceable Obtainable Market (realistic capture). Investors test SOM rigorously.
Target Investor Exit Multiple
Target Return = 4× to 5× Invested Capital within 4–5 years
Standard benchmark for growth-stage VC/PE. The pitch must show a clear financial trajectory demonstrating how the company valuation will grow to deliver this multiple. Early-stage angels may demand 10× or higher.

Shareholder Borrowing Limit
Max Borrowing = 100% × (Paid-up Capital + Free Reserves + Securities Premium)
Private companies cannot borrow from shareholders beyond this cap. Exception: DPIIT-recognised startups are exempt from this limit for their first 5 years.
Convertible Note Parameters
Min Ticket: ₹25 Lakhs per investor · Max Maturity: 10 years
Only DPIIT-recognised startups can issue CNs. No upfront valuation report needed — priced at the next qualified funding round. Requires Board + General Meeting approval and MGT-14 filing.
7.4 · Key Compliance Timelines
Board Meetings
Min 4/year · Max 120-day gap
First board meeting within 30 days of incorporation. First auditor appointed within 30 days; Form ADT-1 within 15 days of appointment.
Annual General Meeting (AGM)
First AGM: 9 months · Subsequent: 6 months after FY close
Form AOC-4 (financials) filed within 30 days of AGM. Form MGT-7 (annual return) filed within 60 days of AGM.
ESOP Cliff & Vesting
Minimum cliff: 1 year from grant date before any vesting
DPIIT-registered startups can issue ESOPs to promoters/directors for 10 years (standard companies cannot). Exercise converts options into equity shares.

Carried interest (VC profit share after returns)
Post-Money Valuation (reiterated)
Post-Money = Pre-Money Valuation + Investment Amount
Preferred instrument in India: CCPS (Compulsorily Convertible Preference Shares). Provides liquidation preference and other protective rights until conversion — avoiding immediate equity-level risk.
Transfer Restriction Rights
ROFR: Founder must match investor offer · ROFO: Founder must ask investor first
Right of First Refusal (ROFR): existing investor matches any third-party offer. Right of First Offer (ROFO): founder must offer shares to investor before the open market. Both restrict share transfers.
Tag-Along vs. Drag-Along
Tag-Along: Investor joins founder's sale on same terms · Drag-Along: Investor forces founder to sell
Tag-Along protects minorities — they exit alongside the founder at identical terms. Drag-Along protects majorities — investor can force a full company sale if a strategic acquirer demands 100% control.
Private Limited Company — Key Limit
Maximum 200 shareholders · Best structure for VC fundraising
Provides limited liability, separate legal entity status, and clean cap tables. Allows ESOPs and FDI. Highest compliance burden but preferred by institutional VCs and for regulatory purposes.
